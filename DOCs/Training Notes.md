Rule 144 US Counsel Explanation

December 3, 2025

12:31 PM

 

Thank you for the introduction, Zafar. I see from the information provided that that there are three different tranches of shares: 7,000 shares issued on January 2, 2024, 40,000 shares issued on May 5, 2025, and 10,000 shares with no documentation indicating the issue date. I note that the physical stock certificates are all dated August 19, 2025. In order to assess the available exemptions to facilitate a legend removal and/or share transfer, please provide the original issue date of the tranche of 10,000 shares. In addition, I need to know whether Mr. Morton is an affiliate of Yangarra. Specifically, I need to know whether he is a director, officer or owner of 10% or more of any Yangarra class of voting capital stock. Also, it would be helpful to understand if Mr. Morton is intending to sell these shares, or if he is only trying to remove the legend to facilitate depositing the shares in a brokerage account.

 

Under U.S. law, Rule 144 promulgated under the Securities Act of 1933 only permits legend removal that is not in connection with a sale or transfer of the subject securities if the shares have been owned for at least 12 months and the holder is not an affiliate. In this regard, the tranche of 7,000 shares issued on January 2, 2024 have been held for at least 12 months and we could issue an opinion to remove the legend if Mr. Mortan is not an affiliate of Yangarra. However, the tranche of 40,000 shares issued on May 5, 2025 have not been held for at least 12 months and therefore would not be eligible for legend removal under Rule 144.

 

Section 4(a)(7) of the Securities Act of 1933 provides for a statutory exemption for the sale of shares that have been held for at least 90 days, subject to certain public information about the company being available and only if the purchasers qualify as accredited investors as defined in Rule 501 of the Securities Act of 1933. As a public reporting company in Canada, Yangarra satisfies the public information requirements of Section 4(a)(7), so we could issue an opinion to remove the legend with respect to the 40,000 shares in connection with the sale of the shares to an accredited investor. Such opinion would only apply to a specific sale to a specific purchaser whose accredited status was confirmed, and the purchaser would receive the shares subject to the same restrictive legend.

 

Treatment of the tranche of 10,000 shares will depend on the original issue date and whether the Rule 144 or Section 4(a)(7) exemptions are available.

 

 

**Response**

 

 

 

Thank you for this additional information. Unfortunately, because he sits on the board, he is an affiliate of the company and the legend cannot be removed for as long as he remaina an affiliate and for a period of 90 days thereafter.

 

Although legend removal is not available to affiliates, he would still be able to sell the shares immediately to accredited investors in the US (in reliance on the 4(a)(7) exemption) or in offshore transactions that occur outside of the United States where the purchasers are neither US citizens nor US residents (in reliance on the Regulation S exemption).  After any shares have been held for at least a year from the original issue date, he would be able to sell shares in reliance on the rule 144 exemption, but could not sell shares in an amount that exceeded one percent of the total outstanding shares of that class in a 90 day period and only if there was current public information about the company available (principally, its most recent annual and quarterly financial statements). The legal opinion for the legend removal under rule 144 in this circumstance would be specific to the transaction and woukd have to confirm the conditions of the exemption were met at the time of such transaction.

 

 

 

Best Execution

Thursday, March 12, 2026

10:59 AM

 

**Department:** Compliance

**Purpose:** To ensure the firm meets its obligations under **FINRA Rule 5310 (Best Execution)**, including monitoring execution quality, identifying deviations, escalating concerns, and documenting oversight.

 

**1. Regulatory Background**

**1.1 FINRA Requirement**

FINRA Rule 5310 requires brokers to **take reasonable steps to obtain the most favorable overall execution outcome** for clients across all orders (price, speed, likelihood of execution, etc.).

**1.2 SEC Rule 606**

SEC 606 requires public disclosure of **order routing practices**, including execution venues and price improvement statistics.

 

**2. Business Context**

- The firm executes orders through **RJLU + Pershing** (service provider).

- The broker provides the order flow; Pershing uses its own routing algorithms to seek the **best possible price improvement**.

- Clients benefit through:

  - Better execution price

  - Faster execution

  - Higher fill rate probability

- Pershing executes **billions of trades daily** and uses quantitative/high‑frequency analysis to improve routing.

 

**3. Oversight Objective**

Compliance must monitor:

- **Price improvement performance**

- **Execution quality trends**

- **Significant deviations or outliers**

- **Pershing’s ability to deliver best execution**

We track **percentage of orders achieving price improvement**.

Normal threshold: **≥ 95%**.

Any sudden deterioration or abnormal spike must be investigated.

**Deviation Rules**

- If results drop by **10–15%**, escalate for review.

- If results suddenly jump from below-threshold back to **100%**, request explanation from Pershing.

- Identify **significant drops** and document rationale.

 

**4. Data Sources**

**4.1 Data Source \#1 – SEC 606 / Route Disclosure (Pershing)**

Pershing provides downloadable data:

1.  Go to [**https://www.orderroutingdisclosure.com/**](https://www.orderroutingdisclosure.com/)

2.  Navigate to:  
    **1‑Compliance → Policies & Procedures folder**

3.  Search for: **“RAYMOND”**

4.  Export the report in **Excel (.xlsx)**.

5.  Save report to:  
    **R:\1 - Compliance\Best Execution\Rule 606 Disclosure**

You may provide the exported file to Copilot if you need automated analysis.

 

**4.2 Data Source \#2 – NETX Execution Quality Scorecard**

1.  Log in to **NETX**

2.  Navigate to:  
    **Tools → Resources**

3.  Search: **“Execution Quality Scorecard”**

4.  Enter the data into:  
    **Excel: Best Execution Workbook  
    **Located at:  
    **R:\1 - Compliance\Best Execution**

 

**5. Quarterly Review Process**

**5.1 Collect Data**

- Download and save updated SEC 606 data (Source 1).

- Retrieve execution scorecard metrics from NETX (Source 2).

- Populate the **Best Execution Workbook** with both sets of data.

**5.2 Use the Best Execution Matrix**

- The matrix checks for:

  - Outliers

  - Trending declines in price improvement

  - Any deviation of **10–15%**

- Run the matrix every quarter in:  
  **R:\1 - Compliance\Best Execution**

**5.3 Identify Exceptions**

If any of the following occur, trigger review:

- Price improvement \< **95%**

- Deviation of **10–15% +**

- Sudden jump to **100%** after a deviation

- Significant unexplained drop in performance

**5.4 Escalation**

If deviations are detected:

1.  Notify Compliance Manager.

2.  Contact Pershing for explanation.

3.  Document Pershing’s response.

4.  Save correspondence in:  
    **R:\1 - Compliance\Best Execution\Correspondence**

 

**6. Documentation Requirements**

- Maintain all analysis in:  
  **R:\1 - Compliance\Best Execution**

- Archive SEC 606 data each quarter.

- Save updated scorecards and deviation matrix.

- Record findings and exception notes inside:

  - **Best Execution Workbook**

  - **Quarterly Compliance Committee Package**

- Store the finalized **procedure** in:  
  **Training & Onboarding Folder**

 

**7. Deviation Matrix Calculation (5%)**

**Purpose:**

To quantify and highlight meaningful variance from expected price improvement performance.

**Procedure:**

1.  In the Best Execution Workbook, find the column containing **Price Improvement %**.

2.  Calculate the deviation from baseline:  
    **Deviation % = (Current Quarter – Baseline) / Baseline**

3.  Highlight deviations ≥ **5%**.

4.  Flag deviations ≥ **10–15%** for escalation.

5.  Document all deviations in the **Quarterly Summary Tab**.
