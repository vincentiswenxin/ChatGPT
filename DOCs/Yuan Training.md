The currently RJLU Portfolio Review Program is still relying on Black Diamond to generated household portfolio data, so there might be some confusion around the number that we are presenting to you.

Essentially our review is based on the client’s signed **RTQ** (risk score/category) vs the **Equity Levels** in the clients’ HH portfolio/holdings.

 

Here is the system breakdown for you:

 

- **Aggressive Growth Target EQ: 93% -\> 100% EQ Exposure Allowed**

- **Growth Target EQ: 78% + 15% Drift Allowance -\> 93% EQ Exposure Allowed**

- **Moderate Target EQ: 64% + 15% Drift Allowance -\> 79% EQ Exposure Allowed**

- **Conservative Target EQ: 49% + 15% Drift Allowance -\> 64% EQ Exposure Allowed**

** **

Please keep in mind that we allow a 15% standard deviation (AKA ***Drift Allocation***) which results in the system above.

** **

To give you more in-depth knowledge, *Black Diamond* does not allow for target ranges/bands (*i.e.,* 70% - 80%).

Therefore, we had to elect a specific percentage for Equity exposures to build our targets and drift allowances, hence the explanation why there are differences between the Equity percentages seen on the RTQs and where the asset class targets lie on the system.

 

In that case, you client has 2 options:

 

1.  **relance** to bring the Equity exposure to just **under 93%,** or,

2.  **to provide a new RTQ** (scoring enough to upgrade the risk level) which hopefully would be able to accommodate the current Equity levels if that’s what they intend to do.

**Documents and Review Process**

1.  **Required Documentation**

    - Scanned copies (front and back) of physical stock certificates, ensuring legibility.

    - A completed Low Price Security Questionnaire (LPSQ).

    - Electronic signature summary (if applicable).

    - Supporting documents (e.g., subscription agreements) showing how the shares were acquired.

2.  **Compliance and Approval Steps**

    - RJLU Compliance reviews all materials and may contact the FA/client for any needed clarifications. LPSQs and supporting documents are saved in our shared drive.

    - Once approved, the Branch Manager reviews all supporting documents and signs the LPSQ.

    - Certificates are then sent to Pershing Custody for final review. Accepted securities are deposited into the client’s account.

    - If restricted, the position appears as **RSTK **in client’s holding.

    - When legend-free shares are issued, RJLU Compliance restricts the account with a note specifying that these shares: Cannot be traded on any U.S. market. Cannot be knowingly sold to any U.S. resident. Cannot be transferred without Branch Manager/Compliance approval.

Under U.S. law, Rule 144 promulgated under the Securities Act of 1933 only permits legend removal that is not in connection with a sale or transfer of the subject securities if the shares have been owned for at least 12 months and the holder is not an affiliate.  In this regard, the tranche of 7,000 shares issued on January 2, 2024 have been held for at least 12 months and we could issue an opinion to remove the legend if Mr. Mortan is not an affiliate of Yangarra. However, the tranche of 40,000 shares issued on May 5, 2025 have not been held for at least 12 months and therefore would not be eligible for legend removal under Rule 144. 

 

Section 4(a)(7) of the Securities Act of 1933 provides for a statutory exemption for the sale of shares that have been held for at least 90 days, subject to certain public information about the company being available and only if the purchasers qualify as accredited investors as defined in Rule 501 of the Securities Act of 1933. As a public reporting company in Canada, Yangarra satisfies the public information requirements of Section 4(a)(7), so we could issue an opinion to remove the legend with respect to the 40,000 shares in connection with the sale of the shares to an accredited investor.  Such opinion would only apply to a specific sale to a specific purchaser whose accredited status was confirmed, and the purchaser would receive the shares subject to the same restrictive legend. 

 

Treatment of the tranche of 10,000 shares will depend on the original issue date and whether the Rule 144 or Section 4(a)(7) exemptions are available.
